FEDERAL POLICY BRIEF
Coverage: 2026-06-29 to 2026-07-06
================================================================

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
-----------------
CMS issued updates regarding Medicaid eligibility verification, alongside a major proposed rule detailing Medicare home health payment rate adjustments for Calendar Year 2027. Proposed rules also cover Durable Medical Equipment (DMEPOS) benefits, clarifying encounter requirements and modifying provider enrollment rules. Separately, USDA plans to consolidate SNAP quality control and disqualification records into a new system while establishing funding through 2031 for the Organic Certification Cost Share Program. Federal activity also includes FDA determinations regarding drug approvals and proposals extending tobacco product registration requirements to foreign owners.

MEDICAID/CHIP
-------------
CMS has issued updates regarding eligibility verification and program requirements for Medicaid and CHIP. One notice announces a new matching program between CMS and the Department of Veterans Affairs (VA) to verify eligibility for insurance affordability programs under the Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act. Separately, documentation addresses specific community engagement requirements that apply within the scope of the Medicaid Program.

A major proposed rule details routine updates to Medicare home health payment rates for Calendar Year 2027. This proposal includes adjustments to behavior factors, recalibration of case-mix weights, and updates to low-utilization payment thresholds. The rule also discusses provisions for home health palliative care services and proposes changes to the Home Health Quality Reporting Program (HH QRP) in alignment with an expanded Value Based Purchasing Model.

Additionally, the proposed rule covers several policy areas related to Durable Medical Equipment, Prosthetics, Orthotics, and Supplies (DMEPOS). These updates include clarifying face-to-face encounter requirements for DMEPOS item replacement, modifying provider and supplier enrollment rules, expanding DME benefits for infusion pumps and drugs, and changing data collection requirements concerning the DMEPOS Competitive Bidding Program.

SNAP
----
The following documents detail proposed revisions and updates concerning data collection, program administration, and specific state-level assistance programs.

Significant changes are proposed regarding how SNAP quality control and disqualification records are managed. The USDA plans to update its system of records by consolidating information on disqualified recipients and quality control (QC) data into a single new record (FNA-5). This modification will cover QC records previously held under an older system, eliminating duplicative records and ensuring continued Privacy Act coverage for program integrity.

In terms of state operations, the Food and Nutrition Administration is soliciting comments on revisions to the information collection required for states administering Disaster Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (D-SNAP) waivers. Additionally, funding has been established through 2031 for the Organic Certification Cost Share Program (OCCSP), which includes provisions allowing state agencies to enter into agreements to administer this program.

General administrative updates are also underway across several areas. The Farm Service Agency is requesting public comment on revisions to information collections used to verify statutory eligibility and secure loan funds under General Program Administration. Furthermore, the USDA has issued notices inviting comments on revising data collection for specific programs, including the OneRD Loan Guarantee Program and the renewal of data required for Build America, Buy America waivers.

CROSS-PROGRAM
-------------
Federal regulatory activity continues across drug, device, and public health data collection fronts. The FDA issued determinations regarding specific drugs, including confirming that Vasopressin was not withdrawn from sale for safety or effectiveness, while also withdrawing approval for 34 new drug applications because the products are no longer marketed. Furthermore, the FDA is advancing its regulatory scope by proposing regulations to extend establishment registration and product listing requirements for tobacco products to include foreign owners and operators.
